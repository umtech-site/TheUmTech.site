Course Overview

This comprehensive ultrasound course provides in-depth training across all major applications of diagnostic medical sonography. You'll learn through a combination of didactic lectures, hands-on scanning demonstrations, and interpretation of real clinical cases.

Dr. Sarah Johnson, RDMS, RDCS

Ultrasound Specialist with 15+ years of clinical and teaching experience

What You'll Learn

  • Fundamental physics of ultrasound and instrumentation
  • Proper scanning techniques and transducer manipulation
  • Normal anatomy and common pathologies across all specialties
  • Doppler principles and hemodynamic assessment
  • Protocols for abdominal, obstetric, vascular, and MSK ultrasound
  • Image optimization and artifact recognition

Ultrasound Probe Types

Convex Array

Low frequency (2-5MHz) for abdominal and pelvic imaging

Linear Array

High frequency (7-15MHz) for vascular and small parts

Phased Array

Small footprint for cardiac and intercostal imaging

Endocavitary

Specialized for transvaginal and transrectal exams

Course Curriculum

Module 1: Ultrasound Physics & Instrumentation

  • Sound wave properties and tissue interactions
  • Transducer technology and beam formation
  • Image resolution and artifacts
  • Doppler principles and applications
  • Bioeffects and safety (ALARA principle)

Module 2: Abdominal Ultrasound

  • Liver, gallbladder, and biliary system
  • Pancreas, spleen, and retroperitoneum
  • Renal and bladder scanning
  • Abdominal vasculature (aorta, IVC, portal system)

Module 3: Obstetric & Gynecologic Ultrasound

  • First trimester assessment
  • Fetal anatomy surveys
  • Doppler in obstetrics
  • Gynecologic pathology

Module 4: Vascular Ultrasound

  • Carotid duplex imaging
  • Venous insufficiency and DVT evaluation
  • Arterial physiologic testing
  • Hemodialysis access assessment

Module 5: Small Parts & MSK Ultrasound

  • Thyroid and parathyroid imaging
  • Scrotal ultrasound
  • Musculoskeletal applications
  • Interventional procedures
